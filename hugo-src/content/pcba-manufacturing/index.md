                <h2>Why Customers Trust a One-Stop PCBA Company</h2>
                <p>Many PCBA customers first try to manage everything separately: one vendor for PCB fabrication, another distributor for components, another assembler for SMT, and another partner for testing. That approach can work for large organizations with mature supply-chain teams. For many engineering companies, device brands and industrial product teams, it creates too much coordination work. When something goes wrong, every supplier can point to another step.</p>
                <p>A one-stop PCBA company simplifies accountability. If solder bridging appears, we can review pad design, stencil aperture, solder paste, placement accuracy and reflow profile together. If a BGA joint is questionable, the conversation includes PCB flatness, surface finish, X-Ray inspection and assembly profile. If a component is unavailable, BOM alternatives can be checked against footprint, electrical requirements and production impact before the order stalls. This is the real value of working with an integrated PCBA manufacturer.</p>
                <p>For customers, trust comes from clarity before money changes hands. XFPCB encourages customers to share Gerber files, BOM, pick-and-place files, assembly drawings, test requirements and target quantity early. With those files, we can review whether the board is ready for assembly, whether the BOM has availability, package or lifecycle issues, whether any components need special handling, whether the panelization supports efficient assembly, and whether the requested test plan is realistic. This early review helps protect both sides.</p>

                    <summary>Is XFPCB a PCBA manufacturer or only a PCB factory?</summary>

                    <p>Yes. For turnkey PCBA orders, XFPCB can coordinate PCB fabrication, component procurement and assembly in one workflow. The more complete your Gerber, BOM, placement and test files are, the more accurate the quote will be.</p>
                </details>
                <details>
                    <summary>What is the difference between a PCBA company and a PCBA factory?</summary>
                    <p>A PCBA company may manage engineering, sourcing and customer service, while a PCBA factory emphasizes the physical assembly line. Customers should confirm whether the supplier controls the actual SMT/THT process, inspection and quality feedback loop.</p>
                </details>
                <details>
                    <summary>Can you source all components for my PCBA project?</summary>
                    <p>We can help source many common active, passive and connector parts. Availability depends on the BOM, approved manufacturers, market stock and substitution rules. For controlled or customer-specified parts, you should confirm sourcing requirements clearly.</p>
                </details>

                    <summary>Do you support prototype PCBA manufacturing?</summary>
                    <p>Yes. Prototype PCBA builds are useful for engineering validation before production. We recommend using the prototype stage to verify BOM accuracy, component orientation, soldering quality, firmware loading and functional test procedures.</p>
                </details>
