                <h2>2-Layer PCB questions buyers ask before placing an order</h2>
            </div>
            <div class="layer-faq-list">
                <article class="faq-item">
                    <h4>What is the difference between a 1 layer and 2 layer PCB?</h4>
                    <p>A 2 layer PCB has copper on both sides and uses plated through holes or vias to connect the layers. This gives designers more routing options and usually supports more complex circuits.</p>
                </article>
                <article class="faq-item">
                    <h4>Can XFPCB quote both prototype and production quantities?</h4>
                    <p>Yes. Send both quantities if you want to compare prototype, pilot run and repeat-order pricing. That helps us review panelization and cost more realistically.</p>
                </article>
                <article class="faq-item">
                    <h4>Do I need ENIG for every 2 layer PCB?</h4>
                    <p>No. ENIG is useful for fine pitch pads, flatness and certain shelf-life requirements, but lead-free HASL or OSP may be more cost-effective for many standard boards.</p>
                </article>
