                    <p class="eyebrow">Engineering fit</p>
                    <h2>When a 4 layer PCB is the right choice</h2>
                    <p>A product usually moves to 4 layers when routing on a 2 layer PCB becomes crowded, when a wireless or high-speed interface needs a more stable return path, or when power distribution and ground isolation are becoming difficult. The extra internal planes can improve layout discipline and make the final product easier to debug.</p>
                    <p>XFPCB works with buyers who need the board manufactured reliably, but also need the quote to make sense commercially. We can review whether your design truly needs 4 layers, or whether the same goal can be met by adjusting the 2 layer design or moving to 6 layers for a denser product.</p>
                </article>
                <article class="layer-text-panel">
                    <p class="eyebrow">DFM and quality</p>
                    <h2>Multilayer reliability begins before lamination</h2>
                    <p>For 4 layer PCBs, our CAM review pays attention to layer registration, drill-to-inner clearance, plane openings, resin flow risk, copper balance and solder mask definition. These details affect not only fabrication yield, but also assembly yield and field reliability.</p>
                    <p>If your board includes USB, Ethernet, RF, WiFi, LVDS or other impedance-sensitive traces, send the target impedance and stackup preference early. XFPCB can discuss practical manufacturing options before your layout is frozen.</p>
                </article>

            <div class="layer-faq-list">
                <article class="faq-item">
                    <h4>Is a 4 layer PCB better than a 2 layer PCB?</h4>
                    <p>It is better when the design needs cleaner routing, lower noise, more stable power distribution or controlled impedance. For very simple circuits, 2 layers may still be more economical.</p>
                </article>
                <article class="faq-item">
                    <h4>Can XFPCB help with 4 layer PCB stackup selection?</h4>
                    <p>Yes. Send your target board thickness, copper weight, impedance requirements and application. XFPCB can review practical stackup options for fabrication.</p>
                </article>
                <article class="faq-item">
                    <h4>Should I define impedance before layout?</h4>
                    <p>Yes. If controlled impedance matters, define the target before layout is complete so trace width and dielectric spacing can be matched to a manufacturable stackup.</p>
