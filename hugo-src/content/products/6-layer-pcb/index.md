                    <p class="eyebrow">Application fit</p>
                    <h2>When buyers should choose 6 layers instead of 4</h2>
                    <p>A 6 layer board becomes attractive when the design has multiple power domains, dense connectors, fine-pitch ICs, mixed analog and digital circuits, or controlled-impedance routing that needs better reference planes. The additional layers can reduce compromises that would otherwise make the board harder to test or certify.</p>
                    <p>XFPCB can help you compare the manufacturing impact of 4, 6 and 8 layer structures. This is useful when the buyer wants the lowest practical cost but cannot sacrifice product reliability.</p>
                </article>
                <article class="layer-text-panel">
                    <p class="eyebrow">Manufacturing control</p>
                    <h2>How XFPCB reviews multilayer risk before production</h2>
                    <p>For 6 layer PCB orders, our CAM review focuses on inner-layer clearance, drill registration, plane relief, resin fill risk, copper balance, thermal relief, solder mask clearance and panel stability. These details are not glamorous, but they determine whether the order runs smoothly.</p>
                    <p>When impedance or high-speed performance matters, the buyer should provide the target impedance, signal layer, reference layer and tolerance. XFPCB can then align the quote with a stackup that is realistic for fabrication rather than a theoretical drawing that is difficult to build.</p>
                </article>
